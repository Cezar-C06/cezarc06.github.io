    The AN/ALR-67 Radar Warning Reciever

    Your RWR (Radar warning reciever) is your most basic tool when it comes to staying alive. It displays all radar signatures currently interacting with your aircraft. The RWR operates on a threat level basis, with the higher threats being displayed closer to the center of the RWR. Each symbol on the RWR represents a different radar type. Most are fairly obvious: 6 = SA-6 29 = Mig29. If you encounter a symbol you don't recognize, do make sure to look it up! As a general rule of thumb, the less beeping your RWR is doing, the better! A series of rapid beeps indicates a missile launch, time to defend!

    Link 4 (AWACs based Datalink)

    The F-14 features a very primative datalink. To the nerds to whom it matters, it is LINK4. This is a signficantly lower bandwith system then LINK16, which is found on most modern American fighters. However, it does get the job done. To access it, open the Jester menu, go to datalink, then tune host, and select an appropiate E-2 or E-3. You can also datalink to ships (most often used for aircraft carriers for automatic carrier landing) but ships are limited by sightline, so they cannot pickup low level contacts more than 20 miles away. Since your radar, the AWG-9 has a tendency to randomly lose contacts, having LINK4 active is very beneficial for situational awareness.
